DOMAIN & RANGE

Domain

- this is the set of x-values in a function.
Ex. {(7, 6) (3,9)}
Domain: 7 and 3

Range

- is the set of y-values in a function.
Ex. {(7,6) (3,9)}
Range: 6 and 9

A correspondence can be classified as:

  1. ONE-TO-ONE
- the relationship between domain and range in which one domain element is linked to one range element.



  1. MANY-TO-ONE
- two or more elements of the domain are linked to the same element of the range.



  1. ONE-TO-MANY
- one element of the domain is connected to two or more elements of the range.

Independent & Dependent

Independent

- it is a variable that stands alone and whose value does not depend on the other variable
- a variable that stands alone and isn't changed by the other variables you are trying to measure

Here are some examples of an independent variable:

1.) Ace is purchasing mangoes for a mango-eating contest at a festival. The number of mangoes he will purchase depends on how many contestants will participate.

In this example, the number of mangoes is the independent variable.


2.) For Jordan to earn extra money during the summer, he works as a cashier. The amount of money he earns depends on the number of customers.

In this example, the number of customers is the independent variable.


3.)Ken has planned a road trip together with his friends to the beach. The amount for gas spent on the roadtrip depends on the miles driven.

The independent variable here is the miles driven for the roadtrip.


Dependent

- it is something that depends on other factors
- it is affected by the changes on the independent variable

Here are some examples for dependent variables:

1.) A person's height is determined by his/her age.

The dependent variable in this sentence is the height of a person.


2.) The organizers of a large rock concert must follow fire safety regulations regarding the capacity of the arena. The capacity determines the maximum number of tickets sold.

The dependent variable here is the number of tickets that can be sold.


3.)Alexandra is filling water bottles for a bicycle race. The number of hours she expects to be on the course will affect how many water bottles Alexandra prepares.

The dependent variable that is presented in this sentence is how many bottles of water Alexandra should prepare.
